The Hidden Homes

They nest in the open. That's what makes them vulnerable.

Ground nesting waders — curlew, lapwing, golden plover, redshank — lay their eggs directly on the ground. No tree. No hedgerow. No wall. Just a shallow scrape in the grass, perfectly camouflaged, completely exposed to anyone who doesn't know to look.

Ireland holds internationally important populations of these birds. But disturbance during nesting season — from walkers, dogs, and vehicles — is one of the leading causes of nest failure.

A nest takes seconds to destroy. It takes a season to lose.

When you see this symbol — pause.

01
Pause
Stop before you step into long grass, rush, or heathland between March and July.
02
Look
Scan the ground ahead. Nests are shallow scrapes — four speckled eggs, easy to miss.
03
Protect
Keep to marked paths. Keep dogs on a short lead. Move away quietly if you see a bird alarm-calling.

The symbol appears on trailhead signs, gate posts, and car park notices across participating sites.
